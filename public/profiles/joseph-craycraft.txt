bio: P. Joseph Craycraft is a distinguished Senior Partner with over 40 years of experience in corporate law, mergers and acquisitions, and corporate governance. With dual degrees from Harvard Law School and Harvard Business School, Craycraft combines sophisticated legal expertise with deep business acumen to guide clients through complex corporate transactions and governance matters. His practice focuses on representing public and private companies in mergers, acquisitions, divestitures, and joint ventures across various industries. Craycraft has particular expertise in hostile takeovers, proxy contests, and corporate restructurings. He serves as outside general counsel to several public companies, advising boards of directors on fiduciary duties, executive compensation, and securities law compliance. Craycraft is a member of the American Bar Association's M&A Committee and has been recognized as one of the leading corporate attorneys in the region. His extensive experience includes representing companies in transactions valued in excess of billions of dollars and advising on some of the most complex corporate governance issues facing modern corporations. Craycraft is frequently called upon to provide expert testimony in corporate litigation and regularly speaks at conferences on corporate law developments.
